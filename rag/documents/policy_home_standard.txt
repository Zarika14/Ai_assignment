1. POLICY OVERVIEW

This homeowners insurance policy provides comprehensive protection for your residence and personal belongings against covered perils. Coverage includes the dwelling structure, attached structures, personal property, liability, and additional living expenses.

2. DWELLING COVERAGE

Coverage Limit: $350,000 (Replacement Cost)

Dwelling coverage pays for repair or replacement of the main structure of your home due to covered perils. This includes the exterior walls, roof, foundation, built-in appliances, and permanently attached fixtures. Coverage is based on replacement cost with a guaranteed replacement cost endorsement included.

3. OTHER STRUCTURES COVERAGE

Coverage Limit: 10% of dwelling coverage ($35,000)

This covers detached structures on your property such as garages, sheds, and fences. Same covered perils apply as to dwelling coverage.

4. PERSONAL PROPERTY COVERAGE

Coverage Limit: $150,000 (70% of dwelling coverage)

Personal property coverage pays for loss of your belongings due to covered perils, including:
- Furniture and furnishings
- Clothing and personal items
- Electronics and appliances
- Sports equipment and hobby items
- Tools and lawn equipment

Special limitations apply: jewelry limited to $2,500, cash/currency to $500, business property to $2,500, and firearms to $2,500 per item. Audio/visual equipment limited to $2,500 per item.

5. LIABILITY COVERAGE

Bodily Injury Limit: $300,000 per occurrence

Liability coverage protects you if someone is injured on your property and sues. This covers medical care, legal fees, and court judgments up to the specified limit. It does not cover intentional harm or injuries to household members.

6. MEDICAL PAYMENTS COVERAGE

Limit: $5,000 per person

Medical payments coverage pays for reasonable medical expenses of people injured on your property, regardless of fault. This includes guests injured during visits. Coverage includes emergency dental care for injuries to natural teeth.

7. ADDITIONAL LIVING EXPENSES

Coverage Limit: 20% of dwelling coverage ($70,000)

If your home becomes uninhabitable due to a covered loss, additional living expenses coverage pays for temporary housing, meals, utilities, and other reasonable costs while your home is being repaired or rebuilt.

8. COVERAGE TYPES: NAMED PERILS VS OPEN PERILS

Named Perils (Dwelling and Attached Structures): Coverage applies only to specifically listed perils:
- Fire and smoke damage
- Windstorm and hail
- Explosion
- Theft and vandalism
- Lightning strike
- Weight of ice, snow, or sleet
- Falling objects
- Accidental damage from heating

Open Perils (Personal Property): Coverage applies to all perils except those specifically excluded, providing broader protection for belongings.

9. EXCLUSIONS

This policy does not cover:
- Flood damage (requires separate flood insurance)
- Earthquake damage (requires separate endorsement)
- War, civil unrest, or terrorism
- Wear and tear, mechanical breakdown, or poor maintenance
- Damage from pests, insects, or animals (excluding sudden damage from wildlife)
- Damage from mold, fungus, or mildew
- Sinkhole collapse
- Damage from nuclear hazard
- Damage from governmental action or confiscation
- Loss due to violation of law or criminal activity

**FLOOD DISCLAIMER:** Standard homeowners policies do NOT cover damage from flooding, including water damage from heavy rain, overflowing rivers, storm surge, or groundwater. Flood insurance must be purchased separately through the National Flood Insurance Program or private insurers. Most mortgage lenders require flood coverage if the property is in a designated flood zone.

**EARTHQUAKE DISCLAIMER:** Earthquake damage is excluded from standard coverage. California, Alaska, and Hawaii residents especially should consider earthquake endorsements separately.

10. CLAIMS PROCESS

To report a claim:
1. Document the loss with photographs from multiple angles
2. Call claims department at 1-800-HOME-CLAIMS within 72 hours
3. Provide policy number, date of loss, type of loss, and description of damage
4. Obtain repair estimates from licensed contractors (minimum 2 quotes recommended)
5. Do not make permanent repairs until approved by adjuster
6. Provide proof of loss within 60 days including receipts and documentation
7. Cooperate with investigation and provide requested information
8. Be present for damage assessment if possible

For losses exceeding $5,000, a full inspection is typically required. Claims are usually settled within 10-15 business days of receiving complete documentation.

11. DEDUCTIBLES

Standard Deductible: $1,000 (applies to most perils)
Windstorm/Hail Deductible: $3,000 or 5% of dwelling coverage (whichever is greater)
Earthquake Deductible: Not applicable to standard policy

The deductible is the amount you pay before coverage applies. Choose higher deductibles for lower premiums or lower deductibles for higher premiums.

12. POLICY RENEWAL AND CANCELLATION

This policy renews annually. Renewal documents are sent 60 days before expiration. The company may cancel with 30 days' notice for non-payment or material misrepresentation. You may cancel anytime with written notice.
